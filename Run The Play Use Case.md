# Run The Play
## Use Case: Why Podcast Advertising Wins, Why It Keeps Growing, and Why the Real Value Sits Below the Outliers

## The Setup

Podcast advertising is the most trusted ad format left in the market. A host the audience already rides with says your name inside a real conversation. No banner, no skip button, no ad blocker. The listener chose the show, chose the host, and hears your brand through a voice they trust.

Most brands still do not run the play. They either ignore podcasts completely or they only know the five biggest shows. That gap is the whole opportunity, and it is exactly what Run The Play was built to close.

## Why Podcast Ads Work

**Host-read ads are a trust transfer.** When a host reads your ad, the audience treats it like a recommendation from a friend, not an interruption. That shows up in the numbers: host-read ads pull 60 to 70 percent ad recall versus 24 to 31 percent for programmatic insertions. Nielsen has measured host-read spots at 71 percent higher brand recall than pre-produced ads.

**Listeners do not skip.** Mid-roll podcast ads see roughly an 8 percent skip rate. Compare that to what happens the second a pre-roll hits on YouTube.

**The audience is massive and locked in.** Around 165 million Americans listen to podcasts monthly, about 55 percent of everyone 12 and up. These are not passive viewers. They picked the show, they finish episodes, and they show up every week.

**Sponsorship beats placement.** A one-off spot is fine. A sponsorship, where a brand becomes part of the show, week after week, is where podcast advertising separates from every other channel. Repetition inside a trusted voice compounds. The audience starts associating the brand with the show itself. That is why the smartest brands in the space stop buying ads and start buying relationships with shows.

## The Growth Story

The money is following the trust.

- US podcast ad revenue hit roughly $2.9 billion in 2025, up about 18 percent year over year, per IAB and PwC.
- The category has grown from $105 million in 2015 to nearly $3 billion in a decade.
- IAB projects podcast ad spend to cross $3 billion in 2026, with growth accelerating from 7.9 percent in 2025 to 9.6 percent in 2026, one of the strongest growth rates of any media channel.
- 71 percent of US podcast creators now produce video versions of their shows, which means every placement now works twice: in the feed and in the clip economy on TikTok, Reels, Shorts, and YouTube.

This is not a niche channel anymore. It is a growth channel that keeps outpacing overall digital advertising while everything else fights for scraps of attention.

## The Outlier Problem

Here is the part most buyers get wrong, and the part Run The Play is built on.

All the attention and most of the money chase the outliers: the top 100 shows. Those shows now command host-read CPMs of $60 to $120. They are great shows, but they are priced like Super Bowl inventory, and for most brands the math stops working.

Meanwhile, mid-tier shows, especially culture shows with deep, loyal, high-engagement audiences, run host-read CPMs of $25 to $50. Same trust mechanics. Same host-read recall advantage. Often tighter audience fit, because a culture show with 80,000 die-hard weekly listeners in your exact demo beats a mega show with 3 million casuals who will never buy from you.

The performance gap between host-read and programmatic is documented. The price gap between the outliers and the mid-tier is documented. The arbitrage lives in between: host-read placements on culture shows deliver outlier-level trust at a fraction of outlier pricing.

Nobody is planning against that. Buyers do not know these shows exist, do not know their real numbers, and have no way to compare them. So the money keeps piling into the same five names while the best value in the entire channel sits untouched.

## What Run The Play Does About It

Run The Play is the urban ad planner for podcast advertising. It does three things:

**1. Puts the culture shows on the map.** Real public performance data on the shows the industry ignores: YouTube views, chart positions, upload consistency, audience size and social pull. The receipts, on display, so a buyer can see exactly what a show delivers before spending a dollar.

**2. Makes the case in the culture's own voice.** Nobody promotes podcast advertising itself. Run The Play does, through content built from the shows and clips people already watch, with the data layered on top. Every piece of content is proof that the channel works and a lead into the planner.

**3. Turns discovery into a plan.** A brand should not need an agency relationship and a six-figure minimum to buy podcast ads that work. Run The Play takes a budget and a target audience and turns it into a slate of shows, placements, and sponsorship options a buyer can actually execute.

## Who This Is For

- **Brands already spending on social and influencers** who want the trust and recall those channels lost years ago.
- **Local and regional businesses** who think podcast ads are only for national brands. A $25 to $50 CPM on the right culture show is within reach of businesses that would never touch a top 100 buy.
- **Agencies and media buyers** who know podcasts work but have no planning layer for anything below the top of the charts.
- **The shows themselves**, who gain a channel that surfaces their numbers to buyers who would otherwise never find them.

## The Bottom Line

Podcast advertising works because trust works, and trust is the scarcest thing in media right now. The channel is growing double digits while ad recall everywhere else collapses. The outliers are fully priced. The mid-tier culture shows are not, and they carry the same trust engine that makes the whole channel effective.

Run The Play is the play: shine a light on the shows, prove the numbers, and hand buyers a plan. First mover on the layer of the market nobody is planning against.
