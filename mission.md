Run the Play Story Model
1. Hero

The heroes are the independent podcasters, publishers, creators, newsletters, websites, and small brands shaping culture without the backing of major corporations.

They have:

Trusted audiences
Local and cultural influence
Strong engagement
Original voices
Advertising inventory
Products and messages worth spreading

But individually, they are often treated as too small to matter.

The culture is built by small players long before big business discovers it.

2. Mission

The mission is to give independent urban media and smaller brands access to the scale usually reserved for major companies.

For media owners:

Make their audiences, reach, and advertising opportunities visible.

For small brands:

Give them a practical way to build a national campaign without needing a national-brand budget.

Core mission

Aggregate the reach of independent culture media so small brands can advertise nationally and the people who built the audience can benefit.

3. What’s in the Way

Advertising access is concentrated.

A relatively small group of large media owners, networks, agencies, and buyers control much of the access to radio, major publishing, national sponsorships, and large advertising budgets.

That creates an unfair pattern:

Major buyers can negotiate lower rates by controlling large budgets.
Independent publishers negotiate alone and have less leverage.
Small Black-owned shows and media companies are treated as isolated inventory.
Smaller brands cannot afford agency retainers or large minimum commitments.
Radio access is concentrated among major ownership groups and gatekeepers.
Audience information is scattered across YouTube, Spotify, Instagram, TikTok, websites, newsletters, media kits, emails, and DMs.
Smaller publishers may have meaningful combined reach but no way to present it together.
Large companies can reach culture at scale while the businesses inside that culture struggle to do the same.
Core problem

Big business accesses the culture in bulk. Small players are forced to negotiate one by one.

That is where the squeeze happens.

4. Who’s the Guide

It does not need to own the podcasts, newsletters, websites, or social accounts. It brings their opportunities together so the market can see their combined value.

Weekly charts and rankings
Educational marketing content
An advertising planner
Campaign baskets
Direct requests to media owners
Guide statement

Run the Play turns fragmented independent media into a visible, plannable national opportunity.

Independent media gains:

Greater visibility

Complete Brand Story

The people who shape culture are often the last people to benefit from its economic value. Independent podcasters, publishers, newsletters, websites, and creators build trusted audiences, but they are forced to operate separately while large corporations buy media, negotiate rates, and reach culture at scale.

Smaller brands face the same problem from the other side. They may have a strong product and a real message, but they cannot afford the agency retainers, minimum commitments, and gatekeepers required to run a national campaign.

Run the Play changes the structure by aggregating independent media opportunities around audiences, budgets, platforms, and campaign goals. A brand can build a national advertising plan across podcasts, newsletters, websites, Instagram, and TikTok while contacting each media owner directly.

Individually, the players may be overlooked. Together, they become a national media network without giving up their independence.

The Run the Play Flywheel
1. Run the Play discovers the podcast

You create a basic public profile using:

Podcast name
Hosts
YouTube and social links
Public reach
Recent episodes
Categories
Available public contact information

The profile starts as unclaimed.

2. Run the Play features the podcast

The show appears in:

Viral clips
Weekly charts
Growth rankings
Underrated-show spotlights
Audience breakdowns
Example advertising plays
Budget-based media plans

The content gives the podcast visibility before it ever pays.

3. The podcast receives an alert

Send a direct message or email such as:

Your podcast was featured by Run the Play. We created a public profile showing your reach and platforms. Claim it free to correct your information, add your advertising opportunities, and control how brands contact you.

The alert should include:

Link to the social post
Link to the podcast profile
Current public metrics
Claim Profile button
4. The podcast claims its profile

Once verified, the owner can:

Correct show information
Connect YouTube and social accounts
Add audience demographics
Upload a media kit
Add rates or contact-for-pricing
Add available advertising formats
Set the preferred inquiry method
Submit clips for possible features
5. The profile improves the planner

The more complete the profile becomes, the more accurately Run the Play can include it in:

Ad Planner recommendations
Campaign baskets
Audience searches
Budget filters
Example plays
Weekly availability alerts
6. Better profiles create better content

The verified information gives Run the Play stronger material for:

New clips
Verified stat bars
Growth reports
Advertising-opportunity posts
Podcast comparisons
“What this budget can buy” content

That content attracts more buyers and more media owners.
